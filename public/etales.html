  • 1 eTales is the first simulation that integrates the Internet-enabled world.
  • 2 eTales is the first simulation that captures the impact of consumer empowerment through social media.
  • 3 eTales is the first simulation that enables continuous interaction and negotiation between suppliers and retailers as they learn to compete and cooperate with each other at the same time.
  • 4 eTales is the first simulation that integrates parallel online and traditional channels of distribution.

Target Participants

  • Senior level managers with functional
    OR general management
    responsibilities at manufacturers,
    especially from strategy, marketing,
    trade marketing, KA , e-Commerce,
    channel management.

  • Senior level managers from Brick-and-
    Mortar retailers and online retailers,
    especially from strategy, merchandise,
    purchase, operation, private label and
    e-commerce

  • Consultants from consumer goods and
    retail research companies

  • Understand the Real Ecosystem

    The new reality of social media, direct access and rising customer expectations in the evolving digital landscape;
    That e-commerce is more than selling and social media is more than communicating;
    The impact of consumer empowerment on modern retail dynamics and the supplier/retailer relationship;
    The challenges and opportunities the new digital world provides for both suppliers and retailers;
    The new marketing paradigm that is emerging, how it will evolve, and how retailers and suppliers will have to adapt to thrive.

  • Competency Development

    How to manage parallel channels in terms of integrated marketing, pricing, assortment management, etc.;
    How suppliers and retailers should compete vertically and horizontally (ie, strategic role of brands, private labels, and online channels);
    How to develop and execute a winning online channel strategy;
    How to build an online business (e-commerce platform and social media presence).

  • Networking

    Bringing together value-chain members in a non-commercial context where they can get to know each other personally and develop a better understanding of each other's environment through possible role reversals.

  • Insight

    Give a perspective on how the industry might develop in the future and how suppliers and retailers will have to adapt to take advantage of the emerging opportunities;
    Provide a frame to test strategic ideas in a rich and realistic accelerated apprenticeship format;
    Identify critical skills and competencies needed in the new reality;
    Help suppliers and retailers think about their respective roles in the future;
    Explore organizational challenges that arise from engaging in e-commerce and/or private label management.

  • Assessment

    A unique and powerful tool to assess management competence and as such identify whom to invest in.

  • Skill Development

    Negotiation skills
    Decision making skills
    Brand management skills
    Time management skills
    Team work and leadership skills

How to play the game

Each decision cycle in the game telescopes a 6-months period in the industry
This session consists of minimum 4 cycles, to experiment with different strategies and decision tactics
Managing customers and suppliers
Model simulating consumers‘ behavior.
